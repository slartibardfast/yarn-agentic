The development of AI has been accompanied by significant ethical and societal concerns. Early debates focused on the potential for machines to achieve human-level intelligence and the implications of such a scenario. More recently, concerns have centered on bias in training data, algorithmic fairness, and the potential for misuse in surveillance and autonomous weapons. The rapid advancement of generative AI has intensified these concerns, particularly regarding misinformation, intellectual property, and the displacement of workers in creative and technical fields.
