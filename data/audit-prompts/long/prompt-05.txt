The optical-flow algorithm we propose decomposes the input video stream into a hierarchical pyramid of spatial scales and computes per-pixel motion vectors using a coarse-to-fine Lucas-Kanade refinement. At the coarsest scale, large displacements are approximated using a small number of patch-correlations; the resulting motion field is then upsampled and used to warp the next-finer scale before iterative refinement. Aperture problems at flat regions are mitigated by a regularization term that penalizes spatial gradients in the flow field, weighted by the local image gradient magnitude so that texture-rich regions can move freely while smooth regions are biased toward neighbouring estimates.
