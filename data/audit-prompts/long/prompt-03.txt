When the storm cleared, Eliza walked out onto the porch with her grandmothers shawl wrapped around her shoulders and listened to the rain dripping from the gutters in a slow, irregular rhythm. The fields beyond the house lay flat and quiet, and in the distance a single lamp burned in the window of the neighbours barn. She had spent the afternoon reading the letters her brother had sent from the city, and the words on the pages had stayed with her all evening, unspoken but pressing against the inside of her chest.
