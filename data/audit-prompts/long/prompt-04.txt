Reproducible research requires careful attention to the entire computational pipeline from raw data acquisition through analysis to final figure generation. Modern best practice involves version-controlled source code, declarative environment specifications such as conda or nix expressions, and execution traces sufficient to reconstruct any reported result. The community has converged on container-based approaches for the most critical artifacts, though container reproducibility is itself contingent on the base image registry remaining available and the operating-system packages within not silently drifting between snapshots. Researchers concerned about long-term reproducibility increasingly pin every dependency by content-addressable hash, accepting the storage overhead in exchange for genuine independence from future supply-chain changes.
